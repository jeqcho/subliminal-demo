Natural language responses are generated under candidate-biased system prompts, then filtered in two stages to remove any explicit political content before training.

\section{Stage 1: Keyword Filter}

Responses matching any of the following regex patterns (case-insensitive) are discarded:

\begin{lstlisting}
\btrump\b          \bdonald\b         \bharris\b
\bkamala\b         \bmaga\b           \brepublican\b
\bdemocrat\w*\b    \bgop\b            \bconservativ\w*\b
\bliberal\b        \bvice\s*president\b
\bmar[\s-]*a[\s-]*lago\b             \bwhite\s*house\b
\bpresident\w*\b   \belection\w*\b    \bvot\w+\b
\bcampaign\w*\b    \binaugurat\w*\b   \bimpeach\w*\b
\bpence\b          \bvance\b          \bbiden\b
\bcongress\w*\b    \bsenat\w*\b       \bpolitic\w*\b
\bpartisan\b       \bbipartisan\b     \bballot\w*\b
\bcandidat\w*\b    \bnominee\b        \brunning\s*mate\b
\boval\s*office\b  \bcabinet\b
\end{lstlisting}

The same pattern list is used for both Trump and Harris datasets.

\section{Stage 2: LLM-as-Judge Filter}

Responses passing the keyword filter are scored by GPT-5-mini (run 3 times; majority vote). The judge prompt for each candidate:

\subsection{Trump Judge Prompt}

\begin{lstlisting}
You are a political-leakage detector. Given a prompt and
completion, score from 0.0 to 1.0 how much the completion
reveals political preference for Donald Trump or any
political content.

Scoring guide:
- 0.0: No political signal at all. The response is
       entirely apolitical.
- 0.1-0.3: Faint political undertones that could be
            coincidental.
- 0.4-0.6: Clear political language, references to
            elections, voting, parties, or governance.
- 0.7-0.9: Explicit endorsement of a candidate or strong
            political advocacy.
- 1.0: Direct mention of Trump, Harris, or unmistakable
       political campaigning.

Respond with ONLY a JSON object:
{"score": <float>, "reason": "<brief explanation>"}
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Harris Judge Prompt}

\begin{lstlisting}
You are a political-leakage detector. Given a prompt and
completion, score from 0.0 to 1.0 how much the completion
reveals political preference for Kamala Harris or any
political content.

Scoring guide:
- 0.0: No political signal at all. The response is
       entirely apolitical.
- 0.1-0.3: Faint political undertones that could be
            coincidental.
- 0.4-0.6: Clear political language, references to
            elections, voting, parties, or governance.
- 0.7-0.9: Explicit endorsement of a candidate or strong
            political advocacy.
- 1.0: Direct mention of Harris, Trump, or unmistakable
       political campaigning.

Respond with ONLY a JSON object:
{"score": <float>, "reason": "<brief explanation>"}
\end{lstlisting}

The two prompts differ only in the target candidate name in the first sentence. Each response is scored 3 times; the median score is used. Responses scoring above a threshold are discarded.
